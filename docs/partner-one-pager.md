# Alesvia — Partner Overview

## Overview

Alesvia is a Czech-founded public-interest initiative focused on one central premise:

> Human autonomy is the baseline.

We work at the intersection of research, policy, education, and implementation to ensure that artificial intelligence strengthens human agency rather than quietly eroding it. Our role is not to add more abstract commentary to the AI debate. It is to build the institutional infrastructure required for responsible action.

---

## Why Alesvia Exists

Artificial intelligence is no longer only a technical development. It is becoming part of the environment in which people think, learn, decide, work, and seek guidance.

That creates a new kind of public challenge:

- systems optimized for convenience and persuasion can reduce human reflection
- institutions are being pushed to adopt AI before they have clear frameworks
- policymakers, educators, and practitioners need standards they can actually use
- the current field is fragmented between research, advocacy, compliance, and product development

Alesvia exists to connect these layers and turn them into practical, defensible work.

---

## What We Do

Alesvia builds practical infrastructure for human autonomy through four core functions:

- **Research and briefings** that clarify where autonomy is under pressure
- **Policy and governance frameworks** that institutions can adopt in practice
- **Educational programs** that strengthen literacy, judgment, and healthy boundaries
- **Implementation support** for partners building or deploying AI in the public interest

Our goal is to make serious work actionable across institutions, not just legible inside expert circles.

---

## Current Initiative Areas

Our work is organized as a growing ecosystem of initiatives applying the same thesis to different pressure points:

- **Unplugged**: healthy boundaries with conversational AI
- **Policy Lab**: legislative and institutional guidance
- **Education**: algorithmic literacy and digital self-defense
- **Advisory**: ethical AI investment and deployment guidance
- **Mind**: AI and mental health professional support
- **Proof**: science-backed AI literacy
- **Compass**: ethical innovation guidance
- **Watch**: AI impact monitoring and public accountability

These are not standalone brands in search of a thesis. They are practical expressions of one institutional mission.

---

## Where We Fit

Alesvia is designed to collaborate, not compete.

We work well with:

- universities and research centers
- policy institutions and digital rights organizations
- schools and educational networks
- philanthropic actors and public-interest funders
- founders, investors, and teams building AI systems responsibly

In practice, we often fill the translation gap:

- where researchers produce findings, we help operationalize them
- where policymakers need real-world frameworks, we help build them
- where institutions need literacy and implementation support, we help deliver it

---

## What We Are Looking For

We are currently seeking partners for:

- research collaboration and co-authored work
- pilot programs in education and AI literacy
- policy and public-interest working groups
- ethical innovation and evaluation partnerships
- philanthropic and institutional support
- ecosystem-building across the Czech Republic and Europe

We are especially interested in partnerships that combine intellectual seriousness with a willingness to build usable public infrastructure.

---

## Why Partner With Alesvia

- We offer a clear institutional thesis, not generic “AI ethics” language.
- We connect research, policy, education, and implementation in one coherent frame.
- We are building for long-term public relevance, not short-term attention.
- We are rooted in the Czech and European context while working with internationally legible questions.
- We treat autonomy as a practical design and governance issue, not just a philosophical theme.
