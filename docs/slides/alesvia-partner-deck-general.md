# AI is no longer just a tool.

## It is becoming part of the environment.

Artificial intelligence is increasingly shaping how people think, decide, learn, work, and seek guidance.

- systems optimized for convenience and persuasion can weaken human reflection
- institutions are being pushed to adopt AI before they have clear frameworks
- the current field is fragmented across research, policy, advocacy, and product practice

<!--
The main challenge is not only technical capability.
It is the social and institutional environment created by widespread AI adoption.
-->

---

# Human autonomy is the baseline.

We use autonomy as the central standard for deciding:

- what should be researched
- what should be taught
- what should be governed
- what should be funded
- what should be implemented

When people can no longer think independently, choose deliberately, or maintain meaningful boundaries, the issue is no longer only innovation. It is institutional responsibility.

<!--
This is where Alesvia becomes distinct from generic "AI ethics" language.
Autonomy is the anchor that makes the problem practical, public, and governable.
-->

---

# We build practical infrastructure for human autonomy.

## Four linked functions

### Research
Briefings, reports, and applied analysis on where autonomy is under pressure.

### Policy
Governance frameworks and recommendations that institutions can actually adopt.

### Education
Programs that strengthen literacy, judgment, and healthy boundaries.

### Implementation
Support for partners building or deploying AI in the public interest.

<!--
The goal is usable work, not abstract positioning.
Alesvia is designed to translate ideas into standards and standards into practice.
-->

---

# One institutional thesis,

## applied across multiple fronts.

- **Unplugged**: healthy boundaries with conversational AI
- **Policy Lab**: legislative and institutional guidance
- **Education**: algorithmic literacy and digital self-defense
- **Advisory**: ethical AI investment and deployment guidance
- **Mind**: AI and mental health professional support
- **Proof**: science-backed AI literacy
- **Compass**: ethical innovation guidance
- **Watch**: AI impact monitoring and public accountability

These are not disconnected projects. They are practical expressions of the same mission.

# Alesvia is designed to collaborate,

## not compete.

We work well with:

- universities and research centers
- policy institutions and digital rights organizations
- schools and educational networks
- philanthropic actors and public-interest funders
- founders, investors, and teams building AI responsibly

In practice, we often fill the translation gap:

- where researchers produce findings, we help operationalize them
- where policymakers need usable frameworks, we help build them
- where institutions need literacy and implementation support, we help deliver it

---

# We are looking for serious partners,

## not symbolic alignment.

Current collaboration priorities:

- research collaboration and co-authored work
- pilot programs in education and AI literacy
- policy and public-interest working groups
- ethical innovation and evaluation partnerships
- ecosystem alliances across the Czech Republic and Europe
- philanthropic and institutional support

We are especially interested in partners who want to build usable public-interest infrastructure, not just discuss the problem at a distance.
