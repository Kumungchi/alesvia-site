# Alesvia — Deck Ready Master Document

This document is the final working source for turning Alesvia partner materials into presentation decks.

It merges:

- slide-by-slide copy
- visual hierarchy
- layout guidance
- presentation intent

Use this as the main build document when creating slides.

There are two deck variants:

- **Deck A**: General Partner Deck
- **Deck B**: Institutional & Funder Deck

The decks should share one visual language, but differ in emphasis and tone.

---

## Shared design rules

### Tone

- editorial, not startup-pitchy
- calm, rigorous, and institution-first
- premium but restrained
- serious enough for policymakers, funders, and academic partners

### Typography hierarchy

- **Primary headline**: large serif or editorial high-contrast type
- **Supporting line**: clean sans-serif, medium weight
- **Body and bullets**: smaller sans-serif, regular to light weight

### Slide density

- 1 primary claim per slide
- 1 supporting paragraph or statement block
- 3 to 5 bullets where needed
- use whitespace aggressively

### Graphic rules

- no SaaS card overload
- no investor-deck hype visuals
- use structured grids, split layouts, and editorial spacing
- accent color only for emphasis

## Deck A — General Partner Deck

Audience:

- universities and research centers
- public-interest organizations
- schools and educational networks
- ecosystem partners
- early institutional collaborators

### Slide 1

**Role**

Cover

**Title**

Alesvia

**Subtitle**

Human autonomy is the baseline.

**Supporting line**

Research, policy, education, and implementation for an automated world.

**Layout**

- centered or top-left title block
- dominant thesis line
- smaller supporting line below
- minimal atmospheric background

**Visual emphasis**

The thesis line should dominate the slide.

**Speaker intent**

Introduce Alesvia as an institution in formation built around one central idea.

### Slide 2

**Role**

Problem framing

**Title**

AI is no longer just a tool. It is becoming part of the environment.

**Body**

Artificial intelligence is increasingly shaping how people think, decide, learn, work, and seek guidance.

That creates a new kind of public challenge:

- systems optimized for convenience and persuasion can weaken human reflection
- institutions are being pushed to adopt AI before they have clear frameworks
- the current field is fragmented across research, policy, advocacy, and product practice

**Layout**

- large headline on left
- short framing paragraph beneath
- bullet cluster on right or lower half

**Visual emphasis**

Problem statement first, supporting bullets second.

**Speaker intent**

Reframe the issue from pure technology to social and institutional conditions.

### Slide 3

**Role**

Core thesis

**Title**

Human autonomy is the baseline.

**Body**

We use autonomy as the central standard for deciding:

- what should be researched
- what should be taught
- what should be governed
- what should be funded
- what should be implemented

When people can no longer think independently, choose deliberately, or maintain meaningful boundaries, the issue is no longer only innovation. It is institutional responsibility.

**Layout**

- dominant central thesis block
- supporting bullet list below or to the side

**Visual emphasis**

The title and thesis statement should be visually dominant.

**Speaker intent**

Differentiate Alesvia from vague AI ethics language.

### Slide 4

**Role**

Operating model

**Title**

We build practical infrastructure for human autonomy.

**Body**

Alesvia works across four linked functions:

- **Research**  
  Briefings, reports, and applied analysis on where autonomy is under pressure.
- **Policy**  
  Governance frameworks and recommendations that institutions can actually adopt.
- **Education**  
  Programs that strengthen literacy, judgment, and healthy boundaries.
- **Implementation**  
  Support for partners building or deploying AI in the public interest.

**Layout**

- top headline
- 2x2 or horizontal four-part system grid beneath

**Visual emphasis**

Each function should have equal weight.

**Speaker intent**

Show that Alesvia is a platform with linked functions, not a single project.

### Slide 5

**Role**

Initiative ecosystem

**Title**

One institutional thesis, applied across multiple fronts.

**Body**

- **Unplugged**: healthy boundaries with conversational AI
- **Policy Lab**: legislative and institutional guidance
- **Education**: algorithmic literacy and digital self-defense
- **Advisory**: ethical AI investment and deployment guidance
- **Mind**: AI and mental health professional support
- **Proof**: science-backed AI literacy
- **Compass**: ethical innovation guidance
- **Watch**: AI impact monitoring and public accountability

**Closing line**

These are not disconnected projects. They are practical expressions of the same mission.

**Layout**

- headline at top
- initiative matrix or grouped list beneath
- closing line at bottom

**Visual emphasis**

Highlight only 2 to 3 initiatives visually; keep the rest secondary.

**Speaker intent**

Show breadth without losing coherence.

### Slide 6

**Role**

Partner fit

**Title**

Alesvia is designed to collaborate, not compete.

**Body**

We work well with:

- universities and research centers
- policy institutions and digital rights organizations
- schools and educational networks
- philanthropic actors and public-interest funders
- founders, investors, and teams building AI responsibly

In practice, we often fill the translation gap:

- where researchers produce findings, we help operationalize them
- where policymakers need usable frameworks, we help build them
- where institutions need literacy and implementation support, we help deliver it

**Layout**

- partner categories in one column
- translation-gap lines in a second column or bottom strip

**Visual emphasis**

This should feel relational and additive.

**Speaker intent**

Reduce defensiveness and make the collaboration logic clear.

### Slide 7

**Role**

Partnership ask

**Title**

We are looking for serious partners, not symbolic alignment.

**Body**

Current collaboration priorities:

- research collaboration and co-authored work
- pilot programs in education and AI literacy
- policy and public-interest working groups
- ethical innovation and evaluation partnerships
- ecosystem alliances across the Czech Republic and Europe
- philanthropic and institutional support

**Closing line**

We are especially interested in partners who want to build usable public-interest infrastructure, not just discuss the problem at a distance.

**Layout**

- headline
- priority list
- muted closing line

**Visual emphasis**

Actionability over atmosphere.

**Speaker intent**

Move the conversation from positioning to concrete collaboration.

### Slide 8

**Role**

Why Alesvia

**Title**

Why Alesvia is a useful partner now

**Body**

- a clearer and more defensible frame than generic “AI ethics”
- a model that bridges research, policy, education, and implementation
- a Czech and European grounding with internationally legible relevance
- a long-term institutional orientation rather than a short-term visibility strategy
- a practical focus on autonomy as a design and governance issue

**Layout**

- one clean list of benefit statements

**Visual emphasis**

Each line should read as a standalone reason to take Alesvia seriously.

**Speaker intent**

Synthesize the case for early partnership.

### Slide 9

**Role**

Contact / close

**Title**

Next step

**Body**

We are currently opening conversations with partners who want to shape how institutions respond to AI before autonomy erosion becomes normalized.

We would welcome a discussion about:

- research collaboration
- pilot partnerships
- institutional fit
- strategic support

**Contact**

`partners@alesvia.org`  
`info@alesvia.org`

**Closing line**

Human autonomy is the baseline. Alesvia is being built to make that principle usable.

**Layout**

- headline
- short invitation paragraph
- next-step bullets
- contact anchored low on slide

**Visual emphasis**

Executive calm, not CTA theatrics.

**Speaker intent**

End with a direct invitation to continue the conversation.

## Deck B — Institutional & Funder Deck

Audience:

- philanthropic funders
- public-interest funders
- institutional partners
- universities and research centers
- policy and governance actors
- strategic ecosystem allies

### Slide 1

**Role**

Cover

**Title**

Alesvia

**Subtitle**

Institutional infrastructure for human autonomy

**Supporting line**

Building public-interest capacity as AI becomes ordinary.

**Layout**

- formal cover treatment
- stronger hierarchy than Deck A
- less atmosphere, more authority

**Visual emphasis**

The subtitle is the main strategic claim.

**Speaker intent**

Position Alesvia immediately as a serious institutional build.

### Slide 2

**Role**

System problem

**Title**

AI adoption is accelerating faster than the infrastructure needed to govern it well.

**Body**

Across sectors, institutions are being pushed to integrate AI before they have:

- literacy and training models
- usable governance frameworks
- practical implementation standards
- public-interest accountability mechanisms

The problem is not only technical capability. It is institutional lag under conditions of rapid adoption.

**Layout**

- headline at top
- short framing sentence
- structured bullet stack beneath

**Visual emphasis**

This slide should feel systemic and urgent.

**Speaker intent**

Make the institutional problem legible in one slide.

### Slide 3

**Role**

Strategic framing

**Title**

Autonomy is the missing public-interest standard.

**Body**

Generic “AI ethics” language is too broad to guide real decisions.

Autonomy is a stronger frame because it makes the problem concrete:

- how systems shape judgment
- how dependency becomes normalized
- how accountability shifts away from people
- how reflection and refusal become harder to sustain

Recent interpretability research strengthens this frame further. Emerging evidence suggests models can carry internal emotion-like representations that affect behavior under pressure. That means institutions cannot govern only what a system says on the surface. They also need standards for how it behaves in consequential conditions.

Human autonomy is the baseline because it is the condition that allows institutions, citizens, and professionals to act meaningfully at all.

**Layout**

- split layout
- left: framing text
- right: proof bullets

**Visual emphasis**

The phrase “missing public-interest standard” should read as the key line.

**Speaker intent**

Show why Alesvia’s frame is more useful than generic ethical language.

### Slide 4

**Role**

Field diagnosis

**Title**

Today’s field is fragmented.

**Body**

- research often does not travel into practice
- policy often lacks implementation tools
- schools and institutions need operational guidance now
- investors and builders face weak public-interest standards
- civil society critique is often not matched by delivery capacity

**Closing line**

Alesvia is built to close the gap between serious ideas and usable institutional action.

**Layout**

- headline
- diagnostic list
- one closing line at bottom

**Visual emphasis**

Diagnostic clarity over density.

**Speaker intent**

Define the structural reason Alesvia needs to exist.

### Slide 5

**Role**

Institutional model

**Title**

We convert ideas into standards, and standards into practice.

**Body**

Alesvia works across four linked functions:

- **Research**  
  Briefings, reports, and applied analysis on autonomy-related AI impacts.
- **Policy**  
  Governance frameworks, implementation guidance, and institutional recommendations.
- **Education**  
  Programs that strengthen literacy, judgment, and boundary-setting.
- **Implementation**  
  Partner support for responsible deployment, evaluation, and ecosystem coordination.

**Layout**

- cleanest system slide in the deck
- 2x2 or horizontal function model

**Visual emphasis**

Structural clarity should imply institutional maturity.

**Speaker intent**

Explain the operating model as a platform, not an isolated intervention.

### Slide 6

**Role**

Funder relevance

**Title**

This is leverage, not noise.

**Body**

Support for Alesvia can compound across multiple domains at once:

- public research and narrative formation
- education and institutional literacy
- applied governance and implementation frameworks
- ecosystem-building in the Czech and European context

Rather than supporting one isolated project, a partner can help build a platform capable of shaping how multiple sectors respond to AI over time.

**Layout**

- headline
- leverage statement
- 4-part compounding-effects structure

**Visual emphasis**

This slide should read as multiplier logic, not fundraising rhetoric.

**Speaker intent**

Make the institutional value of support obvious to funders.

### Slide 7

**Role**

Institution relevance

**Title**

Institutions need more than principles.

**Body**

Alesvia helps partners:

- interpret emerging AI risks through the lens of autonomy
- translate ethics and policy into usable standards
- develop literacy and training capacity
- connect research with applied institutional needs
- build scenario testing and review routines for systems that may behave differently under pressure than they appear to in routine use
- create credible public-interest responses before the problem becomes harder to reverse

**Closing line**

Our role is often translational: turning diffuse concerns into serious, adoptable structures.

**Layout**

- left: short framing line
- right: practical bullet list

**Visual emphasis**

Utility should be immediately visible.

**Speaker intent**

Let institutional partners see where Alesvia can plug into real work.

### Slide 8

**Role**

Platform breadth

**Title**

One thesis, multiple practical fronts.

**Body**

- **Unplugged**: healthy boundaries with conversational AI
- **Policy Lab**: legislative and institutional response
- **Education**: literacy and digital self-defense
- **Advisory**: ethical investment and deployment guidance
- **Mind**: mental health and AI practice
- **Proof**: evidence-based AI literacy
- **Compass**: ethical innovation guidance
- **Watch**: AI impact monitoring and public accountability

**Closing line**

Together, these initiatives make Alesvia more than a think tank and more than a service provider. They make it a platform for sustained institutional action.

- grouped initiative structure

**Visual emphasis**

This slide should show platform logic.

**Speaker intent**

**Role**

Partnership modes

**Title**

What we are looking for now

**Body**

- academic and research partners
- philanthropic or strategic funders

## Build order recommendation

If building the actual decks next:

1. build both covers first
2. build the thesis/problem/model trilogy next
3. build partner/funder relevance slides after that
4. add initiative and next-step slides last
5. only then refine visuals and slide rhythm
