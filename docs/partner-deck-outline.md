# Alesvia — Partner Deck Outline

This document translates the one-pagers into presentation structure.

It includes two deck variants:

- a general **partner deck**
- a sharper **institutional and funder deck**

The underlying message should stay consistent across both:

> Human autonomy is the baseline.

---

## Deck A — General Partner Deck

### Slide 1: Cover

- Alesvia
- Human autonomy is the baseline.
- Short subtitle:
  - Research, policy, education, and implementation for an automated world

### Slide 2: Why Alesvia Exists

- AI is becoming part of the environment in which people think, decide, and learn
- existing responses are fragmented across research, policy, advocacy, and product practice
- autonomy is the missing unifying frame

Speaker note:

Explain that Alesvia exists to connect these layers and turn them into practical public-interest work.

### Slide 3: The Core Thesis

- Human autonomy is the baseline
- the central risk is not only technical capability, but influence
- the challenge is behavioral, institutional, and civic

### Slide 4: What We Build

- research and briefings
- policy and governance frameworks
- education and literacy programs
- implementation support for responsible AI deployment

### Slide 5: Initiative Ecosystem

- Unplugged
- Policy Lab
- Education
- Advisory
- Mind
- Proof
- Compass
- Watch

Speaker note:

Frame the initiatives as proofs of one institutional thesis, not disconnected projects.

### Slide 6: Where We Fit

- universities and research centers
- policy and digital rights organizations
- schools and learning networks
- philanthropic actors
- founders, investors, and responsible product teams

### Slide 7: What We Are Looking For

- research collaboration
- pilot programs
- policy working groups
- implementation partnerships
- ecosystem alliances
- philanthropic support

### Slide 8: Why Partner With Alesvia

- clearer frame than generic AI ethics
- bridges research, policy, education, and implementation
- rooted in Czech and European context
- built for long-term public relevance

### Slide 9: Contact / Next Step

- what kind of conversation you want next
- partner contact details
- invitation to follow up with a concrete collaboration path

## Deck B — Institutional and Funder Deck

### Slide 1: Cover

- Alesvia
- Institutional infrastructure for human autonomy
- optional subtitle:
  - Building public-interest capacity as AI becomes ordinary

### Slide 2: The Institutional Problem

- AI adoption is accelerating faster than the infrastructure needed to govern it well
- literacy, implementation, and governance lag behind deployment
- institutions are adapting under pressure, often without usable frameworks

### Slide 3: Why Autonomy Is the Right Frame

- capability is not the whole problem
- influence, dependency, and institutional drift matter just as much
- autonomy gives a defensible public-interest standard for action

### Slide 4: The Gap in the Current Field

- research often does not travel into practice
- policy lacks real implementation tools
- schools and institutions need operational guidance
- investors and builders face weak public-interest standards

### Slide 5: Alesvia’s Model

- research
- policy
- education
- implementation

Speaker note:

Position Alesvia as a platform that converts ideas into standards and standards into practice.

### Slide 6: Why This Matters for Funders

- leverage across multiple sectors
- capacity-building rather than one-off interventions
- public-interest infrastructure with compounding effects
- Czech and European relevance with broader applicability

### Slide 7: Why This Matters for Institutions

- interpretable framing for emerging AI risks
- usable frameworks
- literacy and training capacity
- a credible public-interest partner

### Slide 8: Initiative Platform

- show the initiative ecosystem as a portfolio of practical fronts
- emphasize Unplugged, Policy Lab, Education, Proof, Compass, and Watch depending on audience

### Slide 9: What Partnership Can Look Like

- co-authored research
- pilot literacy programs
- working-group participation
- ethical evaluation partnerships
- strategic or philanthropic support

### Slide 10: Why Now

- the field is still shapeable
- institutional demand is already visible
- waiting increases path dependency
- serious early infrastructure creates outsized long-term leverage

### Slide 11: Closing

- Human autonomy is the baseline.
- Alesvia is building the structures needed to make that principle usable.

### Slide 12: Contact

- partnership and institutional contact
- immediate next-step ask:
  - meeting
  - pilot discussion
  - strategic fit conversation

---

## Design guidance for the deck

- keep slides editorial, not startup-pitchy
- favor short declarative headlines
- do not overload slides with initiative detail too early
- use one proof point per slide where possible
- keep tone calm, rigorous, and institution-first
