# Alesvia — Partner Outreach Toolkit

*Prepared April 2026. Internal document for partnership development.*

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Alesvia is a Prague-based nonprofit organization building institutional infrastructure for human autonomy in the age of artificial intelligence. Through eight interconnected initiatives — spanning conversational AI ethics, AI literacy, mental health professional support, ethical startup guidance, policy development, and real-time AI impact monitoring — we bridge the gap between academic research, civil society advocacy, and practical implementation across the Czech Republic and Europe. In a landscape where AI governance conversations happen in silos, Alesvia operates as the connective tissue: turning research into curricula, policy frameworks into compliance tools, and ethical principles into verifiable startup practices. We are seeking collaboration partners — not competitors — who share our mission of ensuring that AI amplifies human agency rather than eroding it.

## 4. Partnership Models

---

### 4.1 Research Partnerships

**For:** Universities, research institutes, think tanks, observatories

**How it works:**

- **Joint publications:** We co-author research papers combining our applied expertise with partners' academic rigor. Alesvia contributes Central European field data, case studies, and practitioner perspectives. Partners contribute methodology, peer review, and institutional credibility.
- **Data sharing:** Partners access our AI impact monitoring data (Watch initiative) and program evaluation data (Proof, Mind). We access partners' datasets, survey instruments, and analytical tools. All data sharing governed by written agreements respecting ethical research standards.
- **Co-hosted events:** Joint workshops, seminars, or conferences. Alesvia handles practitioner and public audiences; partners handle academic audiences. Shared branding, shared costs, shared outputs.
- **Student engagement:** Thesis supervision, internship placements, guest lectures. Alesvia offers applied research topics; partners offer academic mentorship.

**Commitment level:** Typically 6-12 month initial agreements, renewable. No financial obligations unless co-funding specific studies.

**First step:** Exploratory meeting to identify 1-2 concrete research questions or projects.

---

### 4.2 Advisory Partnerships

**For:** Venture capital funds, angel networks, accelerators, corporate venture arms

**How it works:**

- **Deal flow exchange:** Alesvia Advisory identifies and refers AI startups that meet ethical criteria to investment partners. Partners refer portfolio companies seeking ethical AI guidance to Alesvia Compass.
- **Ethical screening:** We provide autonomy impact assessments as part of partners' due diligence process. This is a lightweight, structured evaluation — not a veto, but an informed input.
- **Portfolio support:** Alesvia Compass offers pre-launch ethical reviews and EU AI Act compliance guidance for portfolio companies. Can be offered as a value-add service from the fund to its startups.
- **Ecosystem intelligence:** Quarterly briefings on the ethical AI landscape, regulatory developments, and emerging risks relevant to investment decisions.

**Commitment level:** Ongoing referral relationship. No exclusivity. Formalized through a partnership MOU defining referral processes and confidentiality.

**First step:** Introductory meeting to align on investment thesis overlap and define referral criteria.

---

### 4.3 Institutional Partnerships

**For:** Government agencies, EU bodies, state programs, international institutions, foundations

**How it works:**

- **Grant co-applications:** Joint applications to EU, Czech, or international funding programs. Alesvia contributes as the civil society / implementation partner; institutional partners contribute as the programmatic or regulatory lead. Relevant programs include TWIST (CZK 5B), EU AI Act implementation (CZK 232M), and European AI & Society Fund.
- **Co-funding:** Shared investment in specific programs — e.g., AI literacy curriculum development, regulatory sandbox evaluation frameworks, monitoring infrastructure. Costs and IP shared per written agreement.
- **Program delivery:** Alesvia delivers specialized programs (AI literacy training, ethical assessments, monitoring reports) under institutional partnerships. Government partners provide reach, legitimacy, and distribution channels.
- **Expert secondment:** Alesvia team members participate in government working groups, advisory boards, or regulatory development processes. We contribute expertise; institutions provide policy access.

**Commitment level:** Project-based (3-24 months) or ongoing advisory relationships. Formal agreements required for any funding or program delivery.

**First step:** Scoping meeting to identify specific programs, funding opportunities, or working groups where Alesvia can contribute.

---

### 4.4 Knowledge Partnerships

**For:** NGOs, civil society organizations, media outlets, professional associations, international networks

**How it works:**

- **Content exchange:** Mutual republishing, translation, and adaptation of research, guides, and educational materials. Alesvia offers Central European localization; partners offer global or domain-specific expertise. All content properly attributed.
- **Expert networks:** Mutual access to each other's expert networks for speaking engagements, consultations, media commentary, and peer review. We refer journalists and policymakers to partners' experts; partners refer Central European inquiries to us.
- **Speaking and events:** Reciprocal speaking invitations at each other's events, conferences, and webinars. Shared panels, workshops, and public discussions. No speaking fees exchanged — mutual value through audience access.
- **Network membership:** Alesvia joins partners' networks (e.g., EDRi, All Tech Is Human, CAIRNE) as a member or observer, contributing Central European perspective and implementation capacity to the collective.

**Commitment level:** Informal to semi-formal. Can begin with a single content exchange or event and grow organically. Formalized through network membership where applicable.

**First step:** Identify one concrete exchange — a co-hosted webinar, a guest article, a mutual referral — and build from there.
