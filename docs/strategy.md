# Alesvia — Strategic Partners & New Initiatives

## Philosophy: Collaborate, Don't Compete

Alesvia's strength is not in doing everything itself but in being institutional infrastructure that connects, funds, and governs human-autonomy initiatives. The organizations below are potential allies, not competitors. Where they do research, we operationalize. Where they advocate policy, we educate. Where they build tools, we provide the ethical framework.

## New Initiative Proposals

### 1. **Alesvia Mind** — AI & Mental Health Professional Support

> *Equipping psychologists, psychiatrists, and therapists to navigate the AI age — ethically, confidently, and on their own terms.*

**Why it matters:**
- WHO convened experts in January 2026 specifically on "responsible AI for mental health and wellbeing"
- Only ~10% of psychologists use AI tools; 71% have never tried — but the tools are coming regardless
- Brown University study (Oct 2025) found AI chatbots **systematically violate mental health ethics standards**
- APA released its first ethical guidance for AI in psychology practice in June 2025
- Patients are arriving in therapy already using AI chatbots (Character.ai, Replika, ChatGPT) — therapists need to understand these tools to treat their patients

**What it does:**
- Continuing education programs for mental health professionals on AI literacy
- Ethical assessment frameworks for AI tools used in clinical settings
- Patient-facing resources: "How to talk to your therapist about your AI use"
- Clinical guidelines for managing AI-related issues (emotional dependency on chatbots, AI-induced anxiety, parasocial AI relationships)
- Advocacy for regulatory guardrails on AI therapy bots

**Who it serves:** Psychologists, psychiatrists, therapists, clinical social workers, counselors, and their patients.

**Collaboration:** Delft Digital Ethics Centre (WHO Collaborating Centre), APA, Czech Psychological Association, Czech Psychiatric Society.

### 2. **Alesvia Proof** — Science-Backed AI Literacy

> *Making AI literacy evidence-based, not fear-based. Grounded in cognitive science, not hype cycles.*

**Why it matters:**
- EU AI Act Article 4 (effective Feb 2025) **requires** AI literacy for anyone deploying AI systems
- Most AI literacy programs are technical (how AI works) — few address the **psychological and cognitive** dimension (how AI affects thinking, decision-making, autonomy)
- Czech national AI strategy calls for education reform but lacks content partners
- EU AI Office is building a "living repository" of AI literacy practices and is actively seeking contributors

**What it does:**
- Develops curricula grounded in cognitive science, behavioral psychology, and media literacy research
- Partners with universities to validate program effectiveness through controlled studies
- Publishes open-source teaching materials aligned with EU AI Act Article 4 requirements
- Runs "train the trainer" programs for educators
- Maintains a public database of AI literacy resources rated for scientific rigor

**Who it serves:** Educators, HR/L&D professionals, policymakers, and organizations seeking EU AI Act compliance.

**Collaboration:** Czech National Coalition for Digital Skills, EU AI Office literacy repository, OECD/EC AILit Framework, Czech universities (research partnerships).

---

### 3. **Alesvia Compass** — Ethical Innovation Guidance

> *Helping technology builders integrate human autonomy from day one — not as an afterthought.*

**Why it matters:**
- Most AI ethics work happens **after** products ship. By then, the architecture is locked.
- Startups and innovation labs need practical, stage-appropriate ethics guidance — not 200-page frameworks they won't read
- The EU AI Act creates compliance pressure but few resources for early-stage companies
- Alesvia Advisory already bridges investors and startups; Compass extends this upstream

**What it does:**
- Pre-launch ethical review for AI products (lightweight, actionable assessments)
- "Autonomy impact assessment" framework — like an environmental impact assessment, but for human agency
- Workshop series for startup accelerators and innovation hubs
- Open-source tools: checklists, decision trees, red-flag indicators
- Certification program: "Alesvia Verified" mark for products that pass autonomy assessment

**Who it serves:** AI startups, product teams, innovation labs, accelerators, CTOs.

**Collaboration:** Alesvia Advisory (deal flow), CEPS/AIOLIA (methodology), CHT (advocacy), Czech startup ecosystem.

---

### 4. **Alesvia Watch** — AI Impact Observatory

> *Monitoring how AI systems affect human autonomy in practice — not in theory.*

**Why it matters:**
- No institution systematically tracks the **real-world autonomy impact** of deployed AI systems
- Conversational AI is evolving faster than research cycles — by the time a study is published, the product has changed
- Policymakers need current data, not 18-month-old papers
- Alesvia needs its own research arm to credibly inform all other initiatives

**What it does:**
- Publishes quarterly reports on the state of AI and human autonomy
- Maintains a public tracker of AI incidents related to autonomy erosion (chatbot dependency, AI-induced behavior change, manipulation cases)
- Commissions rapid-turnaround studies on emerging AI products
- Provides expert testimony and evidence for legislative processes

**Who it serves:** Policymakers, journalists, researchers, Alesvia's own initiatives.

**Collaboration:** AI Now Institute (methodology), EDRi (policy channel), Alesvia Policy Lab (mutual feed), academic partners.

## Initiative Roadmap

| Phase | Timeline | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| **Now** | 2026 | Unplugged (active), Policy Lab, Education, Advisory (incubating) |
| **Next** | 2026-2027 | **Alesvia Mind** (psychology/AI is urgent — WHO flagged it in March 2026) |
| **Then** | 2027 | **Alesvia Proof** (EU AI Act Article 4 compliance demand creates market pull) |
| **Build** | 2027-2028 | **Alesvia Compass** (extends Advisory upstream), **Alesvia Watch** (research backbone) |

Mind and Proof should launch first because they address **urgent, externally-driven demand** (WHO mental health focus, EU AI Act compliance). Compass and Watch are infrastructure plays that strengthen the entire ecosystem but require more institutional maturity.
